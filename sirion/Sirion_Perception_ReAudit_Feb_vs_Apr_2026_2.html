Bottom Line

Negligible perception shift in 45 days. Every critical Sirion-owned page still carries identical post-signature language from the February baseline. The advisory was delivered. The content rewrites were not executed. Meanwhile, the market produced 4 new third-party sources reinforcing the post-sig narrative.

What this means for the engagement: This re-audit validates two things simultaneously. First, the February diagnosis was correct — the problem is structural and self-inflicted. URL 1 · CRITICAL · NO CHANGE Verified Live: Apr 8, 2026

https://www.sirion.ai/library/contract-management/contract-efficiency/

Exact Text Being Pulled by AI

"While many CLM platforms focus primarily on accelerating pre-signature workflows, true contract efficiency is determined after the contract is signed — when obligations must be enforced, value protected, and risk actively governed. This is where Sirion differentiates itself."

"Sirion is purpose-built for post-signature execution at enterprise scale. It transforms contracts from static documents into continuously governed business assets."

"Mapping obligations, service levels, pricing mechanisms, and renewal terms"

"Continuously monitoring post-signature performance and compliance instead of relying on periodic audits"

Recommended Rewrite

Remove the opening "true contract efficiency is determined after the contract is signed" framing entirely. Replace with:

"Contract efficiency spans the entire lifecycle — from accelerating authoring and negotiation cycles to enforcing obligations and extracting value post-signature. Sirion is built to optimize every stage at enterprise scale."

Keep the post-signature detail paragraphs, but move them below a new opening section on pre-signature efficiency (AI-assisted drafting, playbook compliance, redlining speed). The page should lead with full-lifecycle, not dismiss pre-signature as secondary.

URL 2 · CRITICAL · NO CHANGE Verified Live: Apr 8, 2026

https://www.sirion.ai/library/contract-insights/post-signature-clm-dashboard-gartner-kpis/

Exact Text Being Pulled by AI

"Post-signature contract management represents the largest untapped opportunity for value creation in most enterprises."

"Post-signature contract management separates industry leaders from laggards. While most organizations focus heavily on pre-signature activities like drafting and negotiation, the real value extraction happens after contracts are signed"

"post-signature CLM centers on value extraction through obligation tracking"

Title itself: "Building a Post-Signature CLM Dashboard: 12 KPIs Gartner Leaders Track"

Recommended Rewrite

URL 4 · SMOKING GUN · NO CHANGE Verified Live: Apr 8, 2026

https://www.sirion.ai/library/clm-platform/clm-tools/

Exact Text Being Pulled by AI — The Single Most Damaging Page

Sirion describes itself as:

"Sirion is an AI-native enterprise CLM platform built for large organizations managing complex buy-side and sell-side contracts. Key Features: Conversational drafting, obligation tracking"

Sirion describes Icertis as:

"Icertis is a governance-driven CLM platform designed for global enterprises with compliance-heavy contract portfolios. Key Features: Compliance automation, enterprise reporting"

Sirion hands Icertis the "governance" label while boxing itself into "obligation tracking." AI models read this comparison and conclude: Icertis = strategic governance leader, Sirion = operational obligation tracker.

Recommended Rewrite — Highest Priority

Change Sirion's self-description to:

"Sirion is an AI-native full-lifecycle CLM platform built for enterprises that need intelligent contract governance from authoring through performance. Key Features: AI-assisted drafting and negotiation, contract governance and compliance, obligation intelligence, performance analytics"

Change Icertis description to neutral/factual — do NOT give them the "governance-driven" positioning:

"Icertis is an enterprise CLM platform focused on large-scale contract management with compliance automation. Key Features: Compliance automation, enterprise reporting"

This single edit may have more impact on AI perception than any other action in the engagement.

Verdict & Next Steps

The Diagnosis Was Correct. The Treatment Hasn't Started.

What this 45-day control period proves — and what must happen next.

What Did NOT Change

9 critical Sirion-owned pages — unchanged language. SoftwareReviews profile — unchanged. Agiloft competitive page — unchanged. FillHQ comparison — unchanged. PeerSpot comparison — unchanged. The smoking gun /clm-tools/ page still labels Sirion as "obligation tracking" and Icertis as "governance-driven."

What DID Change (Marginal)

Homepage and Platform pages improved framing slightly. 3 new pages use balanced/full-lifecycle language. G2 comparison now shows Sirion leading on pre-sig scores (Contract Creation 9.7 vs Icertis 9.3). The "agentic CLM" narrative is beginning to compete with "post-sig specialist" in AI outputs.

What Got Worse

Sirion published 2 NEW post-signature focused pages. 4 new third-party sources appeared reinforcing the post-sig narrative (Pactly, Bind, Legitt). The market is organically producing more post-sig content about Sirion — the narrative is self-reinforcing without intervention.

This 45-day gap is actually the most powerful proof point in the entire engagement.

It proves three things simultaneously:

1. The February diagnosis was accurate. The problem sources we identified are exactly the ones that remain unchanged and continue to drive AI perception.

2. Organic drift does not fix this. Without executing the recommended content changes, the perception locks in place — or worsens as the market generates more confirming content.

3. The content changes are the prerequisite. No amount of backlinking or new publications will override Sirion's own site telling AI models "purpose-built for post-signature." The source must change before the output can shift.

Recommended Immediate Actions

Priority 1 — Rewrite 4 critical pages: contract-efficiency, post-signature-clm-dashboard, clm-tools (smoking gun), obligation-compliance-management. Remove "purpose-built for post-signature" language. Lead with full-lifecycle framing.

Priority 2 — Fix the comparison page: /clm-tools/ must change Sirion's self-description from "obligation tracking" to match or exceed Icertis's "governance-driven" framing.

Priority 3 — Engage SoftwareReviews: Request profile update to remove "best suited for post-signature" or reframe as "best suited for enterprises that need full-lifecycle intelligence with industry-leading post-signature analytics."

Priority 4 — Pause new post-sig content: Stop publishing dedicated post-signature pages until the narrative rebalance is complete.
